# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Primary audience is the **broad Beaufort community**: families and students exploring scholarships and programs; donors and supporters evaluating trust and impact; partners, volunteers, and local organizations considering collaboration; and civic leaders who want a credible picture of the Foundation’s mission.

Visitors typically arrive with mixed intent: learn about Rosalie Frazier Pazant’s legacy, understand what the Foundation does today versus what is planned, and decide whether to give, partner, volunteer, or simply stay informed. Context is often mobile, shared via word-of-mouth or community networks, and emotionally weighted (legacy, education, local pride).

## Product Purpose

The Rosalie Frazier Pazant Foundation website exists to **honor Mrs. Pazant’s legacy and make the Foundation’s educational mission legible** to Beaufort and beyond. It should communicate scholarships, future-ready pathways, and disciplined stewardship without overstating current capacity.

Success looks like: visitors leave understanding who the Foundation serves, what is active now versus planned, and how to take a next step (give, partner, volunteer, or contact). No single CTA dominates yet; credibility, program clarity, and pathways to get involved should all feel equally attainable.

## Brand Personality

**Dignified · Grounded · Hopeful**

Voice is respectful and specific: educator’s legacy, Beaufort roots, disciplined governance, and real student outcomes. Emotional goal is trust and quiet confidence, not hype. The site should feel like an institution that earns belief through clarity and restraint.

## Anti-references

- **SaaS startup aesthetics**: metric heroes, purple/blue gradients, glassmorphism cards, buzzword-heavy copy, identical icon-card grids.
- **Generic nonprofit templates**: stock “charity” imagery, vague empowerment language, donation pressure without substance.
- **Flashy marketing**: excessive motion, novelty for its own sake, loud display type.
- **Cold corporate distance**: sterile layouts that hide the human legacy and local community tie.

## Design Principles

1. **Legacy through substance** — Mrs. Pazant’s story and the Foundation’s work are shown with specifics (programs, governance, biography), not generic inspiration.
2. **Honest capacity** — Distinguish active programs from planned expansion; credibility comes from disciplined claims.
3. **Community-first clarity** — Every section should serve multiple community roles (families, donors, partners) without privileging one at the expense of others.
4. **Restrained authority** — Design and copy signal an established, trustworthy institution; avoid startup tropes and charity clichés.
5. **Accessible by default** — Public information must be readable, navigable, and respectful of reduced-motion preferences.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target **WCAG 2.1 AA** for a public foundation site: sufficient text contrast (including on tinted and dark sections), visible focus states, semantic headings and landmarks, meaningful link and button labels, and keyboard-friendly navigation.

Honor **`prefers-reduced-motion`**: any motion should have a reduced or instant alternative. Avoid gating content visibility on animation completion.
